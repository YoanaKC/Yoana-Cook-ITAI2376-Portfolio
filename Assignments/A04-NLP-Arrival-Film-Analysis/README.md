## Overview

A rigorous academic analysis of the 2016 film *Arrival* as a lens for exploring fundamental challenges in Natural Language Processing. Covers lexical ambiguity, symbol grounding, tokenization assumptions, and the evolution of NLP methods mapped to communication approaches depicted in the film.

## What Was Covered

### Three Core NLP Challenges (mapped to scenes in Arrival)

**1. Lexical Ambiguity & Polysemy**
The heptapod word for "weapon" vs. "tool" — the same symbol carries radically different meanings depending on context. Maps directly to word sense disambiguation (WSD) in NLP, where context must resolve which meaning of an ambiguous word applies.

**2. Symbol Grounding Problem**
The heptapods have no shared referent with humans — their symbols don't connect to a shared physical world the way human language does. In NLP, this is why LLMs trained on text alone struggle with grounding language to real-world perception and physical causality.

**3. Tokenization & Structural Assumptions**
Human NLP systems assume linear, sequential language. Heptapod language is radically non-linear — logograms encode meaning spatially and holistically. This challenges every tokenization assumption baked into transformer architectures.

### Communication Methods Mapped to NLP Evolution

| Communication Approach in Film | NLP Equivalent |
|-------------------------------|----------------|
| Symbol-by-symbol matching | Rule-based NLP |
| Pattern recognition across examples | Statistical NLP |
| Understanding intent and context | Deep Learning / LLMs |
